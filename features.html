Features

Tools that go beyond search — course auditing, watchlisting, org visualization, and more.

The Vault

The Vault is After Dark's watchlist. Save any course you need to come back to, add a note explaining why, and access it instantly from the Vault tab.

The Vault — saving courses to your watchlist and adding notes.
  1. Find a course using Course Search.
  2. Click the skull icon on the course card to add it to The Vault.
  3. Optionally add a note (e.g. “Check grading patterns” or “Student complaint — investigate”).
  4. Access all saved courses from the Vault tab.
  5. Click any saved course to open it in the audit view.
  6. Remove a course from The Vault by clicking the skull icon again.
Tip: Vault entries are stored locally in your browser. They persist across sessions but are specific to the browser and profile you're using.

Org View

Org View gives you a visual map of your Canvas instance's sub-account structure. See all sub-accounts with live course counts, drill down into departments and colleges, and quickly understand how your institution's Canvas is organized.

Org View — visualizing your Canvas sub-account hierarchy.
  1. Click the Org button in the After Dark header.
  2. The sub-account tree loads with course counts at each level.
  3. Click any sub-account to expand it and see its children.
  4. Click a sub-account name to search for courses within it.
Use case: Org View is helpful when you need to find all courses under a specific department, confirm a course is in the right sub-account, or get a quick headcount across your institution.

Course Audit

The Course Audit view gives you a deep dive into a single course — grading patterns, discussion activity, content inventory, and usage data. It's the view that opens when you click on any course card in After Dark.

Course Audit — grading patterns, discussions, usage, and content inventory.

What you can see

  • Overview — course details, enrollment count, publish status, term, sub-account
  • Grading — assignment and quiz grading patterns, ungraded submissions, missing due dates
  • Discussions — instructor post counts, participation rates, last activity
  • Content — full module and item inventory with publish status
  • Usage — page view summary, last student activity, most accessed content

Downloading data

Most audit views have a Download CSV option for exporting the data for further analysis or reporting.

Tip: The Course Audit is also accessible directly from the People Compare view — click any course name in a student's enrollment list to jump straight to it.

Demo Mode

Demo Mode scrambles personally identifiable information so After Dark is safe to use in screen recordings, presentations, and meetings. Toggle it on and all sensitive data updates live — no reload needed.

Demo Mode — scrambling PII for safe screen sharing.

Turning Demo Mode on

  1. Click the Demo button in the After Dark header.
  2. All names, emails, SIS IDs, and Canvas IDs scramble immediately.
  3. Click again to restore real data.

Choosing what to scramble

Click the ▾ arrow next to the Demo button to choose which fields to scramble. Fields are grouped into two categories:

  • Identity & PII — Student names, Instructor names, TA names, Emails, SIS IDs, Canvas IDs.
  • Course & Academic — Course names, Course codes, Course IDs, Sub-accounts, Term prefix, Institution name.

Card Designer

Customize exactly what appears on course cards in search results. Show or hide the instructor name, enrollment count, sub-account, term, section count, and more.

Card Designer — customizing what shows on course cards.
  1. Click Settings in the After Dark header.
  2. Navigate to Card Designer.
  3. Toggle each field on or off using the checkboxes.
  4. Changes apply immediately to all course cards.

Themes

After Dark includes 17 colour themes — four generic options and one for every UW System institution. Switch themes instantly from the colour dots in the header.

Themes — switching between colour themes including UW System institutions.

The UW System themes are: UW Oshkosh, UW Milwaukee, UW Stout, UW Green Bay, UW Madison, UW Stevens Point, UW Eau Claire, UW La Crosse, UW Parkside, UW Platteville, UW River Falls, UW Superior, UW Whitewater, and Wisconsin (system-level).

Tip: If you're at an institution outside the UW System, the four generic themes (Sandstone, Greyblue, Coral, Classic) work well for any environment.
